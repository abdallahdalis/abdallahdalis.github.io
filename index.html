The research program

Monetary policy, local labor markets, and who bears the cost

Three installments, one program: a 2,884-county U.S. panel (2002–2019), three identification strategies, and a single question. When the Fed tightens, which local labor markets pay, and how much of what we think we know is an artifact of weak identification?

01

Rate Sensitivity and Employment: A Shift-Share DiD of Federal Funds Rate Changes on County-Level Employment

Compares employment in rate-sensitive counties against less-exposed counties around Fed moves. The raw estimate leans slightly positive, against the textbook story: a puzzle the paper scopes honestly as differential exposure, not a clean monetary shock.

SSRN working paper NABE Mennis Award advance
SSRN · PDF · code · Stata

02

Identification: does the puzzle survive clean shocks?

VAR / SVAR and Jordà local projections with high-frequency Bauer–Swanson FOMC surprises, plus a random-forest benchmark. Under clean identification the expected contractionary effect returns: the Part 1 puzzle is an endogeneity artifact, and this paper shows the mechanism step by step.

Working paper
PDF · code · R

03

Between Metros, Not Within: The Racial Incidence of Identified Monetary Contractions on Local Employment

Once the contraction is identified cleanly, is the employment cost larger in higher-minority counties? The negative gradient survives income and industry controls but lives between metro areas, not within them. Estimates are still imprecise; framed as incidence, not intent.

In progress · June 2026
code & draft · R

Medicaid Expansion & Healthcare Labor Markets

When states expanded Medicaid under the ACA, did healthcare workers' pay and jobs change? A staggered difference-in-differences comparing expansion against non-expansion states, using Census/CPS microdata.

Empirical Industrial Organization

How do firms set prices, and what happens when two of them merge? Two structural projects: demand estimation and merger simulation (Berry logit demand, 2SLS, Bertrand markups, re-solving the pricing equilibrium under the merged firm), and production functions and markups (Olley–Pakes across six estimators on a 2,971 firm-year panel, correcting for which firms survive).

R · structural IO · demand & mergers (PDF) · production & markups (PDF) · code

Market Concentration & Consumer Welfare in Health Insurance

Does a near-monopoly insurer mean worse outcomes for consumers? Using 2024 Illinois Department of Insurance data: the Illinois group health-insurance market concentrates at an HHI near 6,521, with one insurer (HCSC / BCBS Illinois) holding about 80%. The dominance largely doesn't produce consumer harm; claims handling, not market power, drives most consumer friction.

Applied micro · IO & health insurance · paper (PDF) · code
